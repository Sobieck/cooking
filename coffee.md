20 - 22 g coffee

grind finer than pour over, not as fine as espresso

345 g hot water (~1 1/2 cup) (heated for 3 minutes in microwave) 

Fill funnel with measured coffee, level it gently and wipe the rim clean (DO NOT TAMP DOWN GROUNDS)

Screw the top chamber on tightly 

Medium heat

As the water heats, pressure forces the coffee into the upper chamber. You’ll know it’s nearly finished when you hear a hissing or gurgling sound. Immediately remove the moka pot from the heat and let the remaining coffee flow into the top chamber.

To clean your moka pot, wait until it’s cool enough to handle, then unscrew the spouted top and remove the rubber gasket and filter plate above the funnel. Rinse all parts thoroughly with warm water — avoid soap, which can leave a soapy taste. For stubborn coffee oils, use a coffee-specific cleaner like Cafiza to remove residue without affecting flavor. Reassemble the pot carefully, ensuring all parts are properly seated for your next brew
